Welcome! I am a **Ph.D. student in Mathematics** at the **Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech)**, Shenzhen, China. I am supervised by **Prof. Yi Guo** and **Associate Prof. Quanying Liu**.

Before joining SUSTech, I received my **Master of Actuarial Practice** from **Macquarie University**, Sydney, Australia, in 2021, and my **B.Sc. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics** from **South China Agricultural University**, Guangzhou, China, in 2018. 

## Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of **learning theory**, **EEG foundation models**, and **representation learning**. I am particularly interested in understanding how low-dimensional and structured representations emerge in artificial intelligence models and human brain signals, and how these representations support generalization, alignment, and efficient learning.


A central theme of my research is to understand why certain representations are reusable, efficient, and generalizable across tasks, models, and biological systems. This includes studying spectral structures induced by pretraining, scale-invariant properties of learned representations, and low-dimensional mechanisms underlying learning in both artificial and biological intelligence.
